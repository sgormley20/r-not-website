What is Nitrous Oxide

Overview

Nitrous oxide, sometimes called "gas and air," is a familiar medical treatment used worldwide as a fast-acting painkiller during procedures such as dental work or childbirth.

It is a short-acting drug with a long safety record. We are testing whether it can also help reduce PTSD symptoms.

What it is

A widely used, fast-acting painkiller.

Belongs to a group of drugs called "dissociative anaesthetics".

How it feels

Can cause mild, short-lived changes in consciousness, including a sense of detachment, also known as dissociation.

Compared to many other consciousness-altering drugs, effects are usually mild.

Individual differences

Responses can vary from person to person, and even within the same person on different occasions.

Safety

Has been used safely in medicine for decades when administered correctly in a controlled setting.

Why it is being studied for PTSD

Growing scientific evidence suggests it may reduce symptoms of certain mental health conditions.

We are testing whether nitrous oxide alone, or combined with memory recall, can help people with PTSD.
