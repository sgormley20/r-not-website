About R-NOT

Overview

Retrieval-dependent Nitrous Oxide Therapy (R-NOT) is a new approach our team is testing for PTSD.

It combines two parts, recalling a traumatic memory and inhaling nitrous oxide ("gas and air"), in a controlled medical setting.

We are investigating whether this technique can help reduce distressing, intrusive memories in people with PTSD.

Our team is exploring a new and promising approach to treating PTSD. It involves a familiar medical treatment, nitrous oxide, also known as "gas and air."

This drug is widely used around the world as a fast-acting painkiller, for example during dental work or childbirth.

It is a very safe and short-acting drug that has been part of medical care for decades.

The new treatment we are developing is called Retrieval-dependent Nitrous Oxide Therapy, or R-NOT for short.

It has two key parts: the memory recall, or retrieval part, meaning revisiting a memory, and the nitrous oxide inhalation part.

There is growing scientific evidence that inhaling gas and air, nitrous oxide, in a controlled medical setting might reduce symptoms of certain mental health problems.

We want to find out if this is also true for people with PTSD.

In addition, we want to test whether the effects of gas and air are even stronger if participants recall traumatic memories close to the time they inhale nitrous oxide.

We think that recalling, or retrieving, traumatic intrusive memories around the same time as inhaling gas and air may help promote the forgetting of these distressing memories.

More about Gas and Air (Nitrous Oxide)

Here are some other things you should know about gas and air, or nitrous oxide.

Gas and air, nitrous oxide, belongs to a group of drugs called dissociative anaesthetics.

This means it can cause some consciousness-altering effects, including a feeling called dissociation, a sense of disconnection from reality.

Compared to many other drugs that alter consciousness, the feelings of disconnection caused by gas and air are usually relatively mild.

However, it is important to know that the response to gas and air can vary, both from person to person, and even within the same person on different occasions.
