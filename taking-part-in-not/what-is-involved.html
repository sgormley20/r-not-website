What is Involved

Overview

Taking part in this study involves several stages, including an online screening call, preparation and treatment visits, and follow-up monitoring at home.

Step 1 – Online Screening Call

  • Conducted via phone or MS Teams, no travel needed
  • Lasts about 1.5 hours
  • We will ask about your health, medications, PTSD symptoms, and intrusive memories
  • Your answers are confidential and secure
  • If eligible, you will be invited to the preparation visit

Step 2 – Preparation Visit

  • In-person at UCL, approximately 2 hours
  • Helps you get ready for the treatment visit
  • Identify memories to focus on during treatment
  • Complete baseline questionnaires and medical checks, including height and weight
  • Learn how gas and air works and practise grounding techniques if it feels overwhelming
  • Only brief summaries of memories are needed, no detailed descriptions
  • At the end, we schedule your treatment visit

Step 3 – Treatment Visit

  • One in-person visit at UCLH, approximately 3.5 hours
  • Focuses on your most distressing memory
  • Briefly recall the memory, inhale gas and air for one hour, and be monitored before, during, and after
  • You have full control over inhalation and can take breaks or stop if needed
  • After inhalation, you rest and recover before leaving

Step 4 – Monitoring Symptoms at Home

  • Complete a short daily online diary about intrusive memories, including frequency, vividness, and distress
  • Wear a smartwatch to measure heart rate and record memories as they occur
  • Occasional online questionnaires
  • Support is provided to help you complete these tasks

Step 5 – Follow-Up Calls

  • Conducted 7 and 28 days after your treatment via phone or MS Teams
  • We will check your PTSD and mental health symptoms
  • Return the smartwatch in a pre-paid envelope
