What is Involved?

Taking part in this study will involve an online screening call, an assessment and preparation visit, a treatment visit, and follow-up monitoring at home/online.

Online Screening Call

  • This will be conducted confidentially and securely via phone or MS Teams, there is no need to travel to us.
  • It will last about 2 hours.
  • We will ask about your health, medications, PTSD symptoms, and intrusive memories.
  • If eligible, you will be invited to the assessment and preparation visit.

Assessment and Preparation Visit

  • You will attend an in-person visit at University College London.This visit is approximately 2 hours.
  • This visit will help you get ready for the treatment visit.
  • You will identify memories to focus on during treatment.
  • You will complete baseline questionnaires and a health check.
  • You will learn about the treatment and some basic techniques for managing distress.

Treatment Visit

  • You will attend one in-person visit at the Clinical Research Facilities at University College London Hospital. The visit will last approximately 3 hours.
  • You will recall some neutral (non-emotional) and traumatic memories and inhale nitrous oxide for one hour. We will monitor how you are feeling throughout the session.
  • After you have had some time to rest and recover, we will ensure you are ready to leave the unit.

Monitoring Symptoms at Home

  • You will be asked you to complete a short online diary about intrusive memories every day for one week. This will only take a few minutes at the end of the day.
  • You will be asked you to wear a smartwatch to record your symptoms as they occur.
  • You will be asked to complete a small number of short online questionnaires.
  • These measurements will help us track whether R-NOT is working for you in the expected way.
  • Support will be provided to help you complete these tasks, if you need it.

Follow-Up Call

  • This will take place 7 after your treatment via phone or MS Teams.
  • We will check your PTSD and mental health symptoms.
