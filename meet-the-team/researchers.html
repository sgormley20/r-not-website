About the Team Meet the Team

Sunjeev Kamboj

Professor at the Clinical Psychopharmacology Unit

Professor Sunjeev Kamboj is the principal investigator and project lead for the R-NOT project. He is a clinical psychologist and cognitive therapist and has worked with people with PTSD for more than 25 years. His research aims to develop new, effective medications and psychological therapies for PTSD and related problems.

Siobhan Gormley

Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Clinical Psychopharmacology Unit

Dr Gormley is a Postdoctoral Research fellow on the R-NOT project. She is a mental health researcher with a decade of experience in the field. Her work focuses on supporting the delivery of this clinical trial for PTSD and contributing to the development of improved treatments.

Alexandre Piot

PhD Student at Clinical Psychopharmacology Unit

Alex is a PhD student at UCL's Clinical Psychopharmacology Unit, where his research explores the role of dissociative states in shaping how people relate to themselves. His work sits at the intersection of altered states of consciousness, neuroscience, and mental health — an intersection that sits at the heart of the R-NOT trial. Alex holds degrees from UCL in Experimental Pharmacology and Therapeutics and Clinical Neuroscience. Previously he was a Research Fellow at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, where he worked on a harm reduction intervention to improve health outcomes and service engagement in a marginalised population. On the R-NOT trial, Alex is involved in participant recruitment and data collection, and is committed to ensuring that participants feel informed, respected, and supported throughout their involvement.

Anne Marie Downey

Head of Clinical Trials Operations at PRIMENT

Anne Marie Downey is the Head of Clinical Trials Operations at PRIMENT Clinical Trials Unit, where she leads the strategic and operational delivery of complex clinical trial projects in primary care and mental health. Anne Marie has over 24 years experience in clinical trial operations with extensive knowledge in the clinical trial regulations and other research governance frameworks. She leads multidisciplinary teams, foster collaboration, and successfully drives the operational delivery of several high-quality clinical trials, including the R-NOT trial.

Irwin Nazareth

Professor at PRIMENT

Irwin Nazareth is Professor of Primary Care & Population Sciences and Director of PRIMENT CTU at UCL. He has special expertise in design and evaluation of complex intervention in mental health. As a trialist on R-NOT offers support on the design, conduct, methodology, and evaluation of the trial.

Georges Iskander

Consultant Anaesthetist

Consultant Anaesthetist and Lead for Visiting Doctors at UCLH; Honorary Lecturer in Clinical Education and Health Psychology at UCL. Chief Investigator of the R-NOT trial, with clinical and research interests in Endocrine Anaesthesia, Perioperative Medicine, and Psychopharmacology. On a personal level, I serve as a deacon in the Church of England and speak English, Arabic, Italian, and French.

Jonathon Bisson

Professor, Division of Psychological Medicine and Clinical Neurosciences

Jon is a practising psychiatrist and Clinical Professor in Psychiatry at Cardiff University. He is Director of the National Centre for Mental Health and leads its intervention development workstream and Cardiff University’s Traumatic Stress Research Group. Jon has conducted many research studies, including ten randomised controlled trials and five Cochrane systematic reviews that have shaped research and practice in the traumatic stress field.
