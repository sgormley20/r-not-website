Meet the Patient Advisory Group

Joanne Lloyd

PAG Chair

As Chair of the Patient Advisory Group, my role is to help ensure that people with a personal understanding of PTSD are involved throughout the project. This includes reviewing participant information and consent materials, paying close attention to language, and raising questions about emotional impact, safety, and how the study may feel from a participant’s perspective. The PAG provides a space where patient insight can shape how the research is designed and delivered, helping the team think carefully about acceptability, clarity, and support.

Experience in patient and public involvement
Alongside this personal understanding, I bring several years’ experience in patient and public involvement (PPIE). I’ve worked with research teams to help shape study design, strengthen patient-facing materials, and support approaches to involvement that are built into decision-making and followed through in practice. I also have experience contributing to research ethics discussions and working with research teams to consider how studies affect people from socially and economically disadvantaged backgrounds. This has shaped my focus on fairness, accessibility, and ensuring research is designed in ways that do not unintentionally exclude or place additional burdens on people who already face barriers to care.

Gareth Gault

PAG Member

I've had PTSD for years but only diagnosed in recent years. My career background is primarily [30 years] in the third sector and social enterprises - across national and regional charities but also local government, health and education.

Due to this, as well as some other long term physical health issues, i'm passionate about ampliphying the patient voice and including lived experience in research. Therefore , I’ve become involved in coproduction, amplifying the voice of lived experience, patient and public voice & embedding it at all levels.

I’m mainly involved in research and data spaces , as a public and / or patient member , but also specialist committees with NICE, NIHR and others nationally, as well as health & social care data committees on a more regional level too.
